Goals for 2025/2026

Building on this year's artefacts, three priorities run through my goals: writing more complex academic work, developing the English I need for teaching, and reading denser academic and technical texts.

Writing

Building on my research paper, I want to become genuinely comfortable with complex academic writing. My goal is to produce at least two longer academic pieces this semester - for example a literature review or a research-style report - and to work on exactly what the paper exposed: holding an academic register the whole way through, hedging claims precisely, citing sources in one consistent style, and cutting my over-long sentences. Concretely, I will revise each piece at least once specifically for register and sentence length.

Reading

After Orlando and The Time Machine, I want to move from fiction and journalism toward denser academic and technical literature - research articles and methodology texts in education. My goal is to read and annotate at least one academic article every two weeks, keep a running glossary of technical and academic vocabulary, and summarise each text in a few sentences to check that I have actually understood it.

Listening

Listening is the skill I documented least this year, so my main goal is to turn informal listening into real artefacts. My plan is to take structured notes on an English podcast or lecture at least once a week, and to design one listening task I could use in teaching. I will focus on academic and teaching-related talks, such as methodology or ELT presentations, so this also feeds my teaching goal.

Speaking

The feedback on my presentation made my priority clear: vary my delivery and build the spoken English I need in the classroom. My goal is to record at least two more short presentations or explanations this semester, each time marking stress and pauses in the script beforehand and deliberately varying tone and pace. I also want to grow my teaching vocabulary - instructions, feedback phrases, classroom-management language - and actually use it when I tutor.
